The Phillipian Endowment

Securing the future of student journalism

Welcome to The Newsroom

For 136 years, The Phillipian has served as a platform for student voices and an informant in campus discussion. Two years ago, the paper broke the story of John Palfrey's selection as the latest Head of School. Last spring, we published a series of Letters to the Editor sparking a campus feminist movement that has gained national attention. Recently, we have run in-depth articles examining Andover's declining acceptance rate, the extent of mobility between our Varsity and Junior Varsity sports teams, and the evolving conversation on diversity at Andover. We have extended beyond campus borders to co-byline stories with a journalism class at Lawrence High School that tear down misconceptions and showcase the opinions of students from both schools.

Arguably more important than its public role as a newspaper, however, The Phillipian also serves as the largest, most energetic student classroom on campus. Each week, the paper embodies the work of over one hundred students who disperse across campus to find and write hard-hitting news and commentary, edit each others' writing, and pull together a 12 to 16 page newspaper. We, as students, drive and own every step of the process, from thinking of story ideas to placing the final lines on the front page. We brainstorm and we discuss and we argue. We teach and we learn from each other.

With the 2008 economic downturn, The Phillipian - like many local and national news outlets - faced financial difficulties. Despite strong support from students, alumni, and parents, we have barely broken even in the past several years. To secure The Phillipian's future in perpetuity - both in print and online - we, the editorial board, now seek to fund a $500,000 endowment.

"There is not a single class at Andover where I could learn team management, fiscal planning, customer service, writing technique, and time budgeting all at once. The newsroom truly is Andover's greatest professional laboratory."

-Stephen Moreland '14, Editor in Chief

Income from the endowment will upgrade our online presence, renew technology and equipment, enable on- and off-campus opportunities for future boards, decrease dependence on paid subscriptions, and stabilize The Phillipian by covering a portion of its expenses. We will, of course, continue to manage the paper like a small business by generating advertising revenue and producing each issue within an annual budget.

The Phillipian's annual operating budget averages $55,000, most of which is spent on printing and mailing. Until recently, subscriptions purchased by students, parents, and alumni have covered the bulk of these costs. But with an unprecedented 47 percent of students on financial aid, the Phillipian subscription fee has become too much for many families to bear; income from subscriptions continues to fall.

To foster the ongoing success of The Phillipian while respecting its independence, the Academy is seeking to establish a $500,000 endowment, which would generate approximately $20,000 to $25,000 annually.

The Phillipian Makes The Move Online

With over 40,000 unique visitors each month, The Phillipian has taken significant strides to increasing and maintaining a robust online and social media presence. We now operate a website, online image gallery, digital archives, and Twitter and Facebook accounts with thousands of followers. Our Sports editors (@PhillipianSprts) have over three hundred followers, and make use of an innovative system of tweeting and re-tweeting live coverage of nearly every home sport event from our associates and writers. Arts & Leisure has also taken well to the move to social media, sending reporters out to weekend gallery events and concerts equipped with iPhones and voice recorders in order to bring back multimedia to incorporate into online articles or fodder for The Phillipian's active Instagram account.

Perhaps most significantly, the new focus online has engendered a larger shift in the foundations of the paper's weekly workflow. With the advent of a more active online presence, we now train our associates and writers to operate on a near 24-hour news schedule. No longer do we wait until Friday to upload the week's articles; we break our most pressing stories online. This fall, for example, we covered a change in the school's dance dress code policy online as it broke. Our editors worked through an entire Saturday to publish the article graf by graf, tweeting updates as we interviewed and clarified the new rules with our sources. The piece gained 3,000 views over the course of a day. Students responded - voiced their support or, more often, outrage - via Twitter and Facebook in real time. In turn, we published many of these responses as embedded quotes in the actual article.

All in all, social media and the advent of online have changed the way we report the news. It has changed the way every newspaper reports the news. These technologies, however, are constantly changing. Thus the endowment will play a crucial role in making the transition to a digital-first newspaper in a digital-first world. Income from the endowment will support, most notably:

  • Website redesign - to include a crawl that pulls from all Phillipian Twitter feeds and a complete front-end redesign that will streamline the online reader experience
  • New iPhone App - up to date with iOS 7 design schemes; will bring recent articles to iPhones for on- and off-line viewing
  • Escalated presence on Twitter - to facilitate timelier online reporting
  • Greater use of Facebook - to promote article, surveys, and events

Off-Campus Learning Experiences

The Phillipian staff periodically attends journalism conferences, such as:

  • The National Scholastic Press Association/Journalism Education Association National Conference in Washington, D.C., and Seattle
  • The Columbia University Scholastic Press Association Convention
  • The New England Scholastic Press Association Conference at Boston University

Staffers also travel to meetings with editors at media outlets such as The Washington Post, The Atlantic, Newsweek, The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, The New York Daily News, Vogue magazine, The New York Post, The New Yorker, Forbes, CBS, ABC News, Fox News, and Facebook, and visit CUNY's entrepreneurial journalism program.
